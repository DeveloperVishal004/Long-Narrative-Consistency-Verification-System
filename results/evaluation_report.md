Generated by `scripts/evaluate_dataset.py`, run against the real, unmodified

`sentence-transformers/all-MiniLM-L6-v2` embedder, real
`cross-encoder/nli-deberta-v3-base` NLI model, real retrieval, real RRF

metrics. Raw per-claim outputs are at `results/predictions_train.jsonl` and

**Class imbalance:** moderate (63.75% / 36.25%), not severe.

**Data quality issue found:** many `content` strings are pronoun-only ("He once found bribery entries...") with no character name in the sentence — `char` is required external context. Resolved in this study by constructing `original_claim = f"Regarding {char}: {content}"`.

**Suitability:**
- **Verdict evaluation: train.csv only.** test.csv has no gold labels and cannot be scored.
- **Retrieval evaluation: not possible.** No evidence-span/chunk annotations exist in either file. Recall@k, MRR, and gold-grounded citation accuracy are **not computed** — reporting them would be fabrication. This report shows actual retrieved/fused evidence per claim instead (Section 6), which is auditable but not a scored metric.

**(precision/recall columns omitted above are in `evaluation_analysis.json`; both are flat across the sweep at the same resolution as accuracy/macro F1 — no threshold produces a materially different result.)**

**Best accuracy: 0.338, tied across all 7 thresholds. Best macro F1: 0.194, tied across 0.65–0.80.** Raising `contradiction_threshold` to 0.80 — 60% higher than the established default — changes essentially nothing.

**Why the sweep is flat (verified directly, not inferred):** the false-positive contradiction scores and the true-positive contradiction scores are nearly indistinguishable:

| Gold label | Claims with ≥1 CONTRADICTION result | Mean of each claim's max contradiction score | Minimum observed |
|---|---|---|---|
| consistent (false positives) | 39/51 | 0.978 | 0.617 |
| contradict (true positives) | 27/29 | 0.991 | 0.858 |

A threshold cannot separate two distributions that overlap this tightly at the top end. This directly falsifies the long-narrative validation's working hypothesis that `contradiction_threshold=0.5` being "too permissive" was the dominant problem — **on this dataset, the false positives are confident enough that no tested threshold (up to 0.80) filters them out.**

## 5. Error Analysis (Phase D)

**53/80 (66%) incorrect on train.csv. 100% of these classified as NLI-layer failures** by examining each: in every incorrect case, evidence *was* retrieved and fused (0 claims had empty `fused_evidence` or `nli_results` — retrieval/fusion never failed to produce candidates), so the failure is downstream of retrieval, in how NLI scores what was retrieved.

**The single most important, fully-verified fact in this study:**

> **Across all 800 real NLI calls on train.csv, the model assigned the label `ENTAILMENT` exactly zero times.** Label distribution: NEUTRAL=568 (71%), CONTRADICTION=232 (29%), ENTAILMENT=0 (0%).

This makes `CONSISTENT` **structurally unreachable on this dataset at any threshold setting** — it is not a calibration problem, it is the complete absence of one of the three NLI signals ever firing. This is the dominant error source, not retrieval, not fusion, and not threshold calibration in the sense Phase E was designed to test.

**Why ENTAILMENT never fires (supported by the examples below):** `content` rows are often elaborate, specific, invented biographical embellishments ("he studied the crack-patterns of his mother's shark-tooth necklace by moonlight") that no single 700-character passage of the real 19th-century novel text states or directly implies — true entailment requires the evidence to actually assert the claim, which an invented elaboration cannot satisfy by construction, regardless of how "in the spirit of the character" the dataset's `consistent` label considers it to be. This is a **task-definition mismatch**: the dataset's notion of "consistent" (loosely fits the character) is looser than LNCVS's notion (the source text actually entails the specific claim).

**Compounding factor — topically irrelevant retrieval on specific/rare claims:** the two lowest-confidence false positives found (used as the clearest examples below) show retrieval surfacing evidence about a *different character entirely*.

### Failure mode breakdown

| Failure mode | Count | % of incorrect |
|---|---|---|
| NLI (wrong/missing label given the retrieved evidence) | 53 | 100% |
| Retrieval (zero evidence reached NLI) | 0 | 0% |
| Threshold (right label present, just below cutoff) | 0 | 0% |
| Rule-engine (verdict didn't follow from the recorded classification) | 0 | 0% |

**Dominant error source: NLI** — specifically, (a) `ENTAILMENT` never fires at all, and (b) `CONTRADICTION` fires with high confidence against evidence that is often topically adjacent but not actually conflicting, sometimes about the wrong character entirely. Retrieval and fusion always produced candidates (never the empty-evidence case); the rule engine correctly applied its dominance rule to whatever NLI handed it — the breakdown is upstream of the rule engine, in the NLI layer's interaction with this claim style.

## 6. Failure Examples (real data, real pipeline output)

**False positive contradiction, lowest confidence found (0.617) — wrong character retrieved:**
> Claim: *"Regarding Thalcave: He briefly loved Chilean doctor Mariana, who wanted him to settle down but was refused; she died nursing cholera-stricken Indians."*
> Gold: `consistent` → Predicted: `CONTRADICTORY` (score 0.617)
> Cited premise: *"...young creature, high-minded and devoted, the lord of Luss had made the companion of his life. He found her one day living alone, an orphan..."* — **this passage is about Lord Glenarvan's backstory, not Thalcave.**

**False positive contradiction, second-lowest (0.642) — same pattern:**
> Claim: *"Regarding Thalcave: Early trauma: at seven his father vanished in a local uprising, at nine his mother died of illness; a banished Jesuit, Father Lecl[erc]..."*
> Gold: `consistent` → Predicted: `CONTRADICTORY` (score 0.642)
> Cited premise: discusses a Scottish minister's son — again, unrelated to Thalcave.

**Genuine, correct contradiction (the system working as intended):**
> Claim: *"Regarding Faria: Suspected again in 1815, he was re-arrested and shipped to the Château d'If, this time for life."*
> Gold: `contradict` → Predicted: `CONTRADICTORY` (score 0.996–1.000)
> Cited premise: real Monte Cristo text establishing Faria's actual, different imprisonment circumstances. **This is the dominant pattern for true contradictions — when the claim's specific fictional detail genuinely conflicts with retrievable real text, the system finds it reliably (93.1% recall).**

**High-confidence false positive on an elaborate invented claim:**
> Claim: *"Regarding Kai-Koumou: Before each fight he studied the crack-patterns of his mother's shark-tooth necklace by moonlight to divine the outcome."*
> Gold: `consistent` → Predicted: `CONTRADICTORY` (score up to 1.000 across multiple retrieved chunks)
> Retrieved evidence is real Kai-Koumou narrative content (tribal warfare, captives, taboo customs) — topically correct character, but none of it confirms or denies this invented divination detail. NLI assigned `CONTRADICTION` rather than the correct `NEUTRAL` four separate times.

## 7. Recommended Threshold

**No threshold in the tested range (0.50–0.80) meaningfully changes the outcome on this dataset.** If forced to choose, **0.65–0.80** gives a marginally better macro F1 (0.194 vs 0.189) at no cost to accuracy, so **0.65** is the recommended setting if one must be picked — it gets the small available benefit without moving further from the validated 0.50 default than necessary. **This is a weak, low-confidence recommendation**: the real lever for this dataset is not the contradiction threshold.

## 8. Recommended Next Steps

1. **Investigate why `ENTAILMENT` never fires.** This is the single highest-leverage finding. Candidates to test (analysis only, no architecture change made here): whether `cross-encoder/nli-deberta-v3-base` is simply miscalibrated for "elaborate biographical claim vs. narrative paragraph" pairs vs. its training distribution (typically short, direct sentence pairs); whether multi-sentence compound claims need decomposition into single-fact atomic claims (this study used one atomic claim per row, by necessity, since no real LLM decomposer exists — see Phase A) before NLI, rather than NLI judging a 2–3 sentence compound claim against a single passage at once.
2. **Reconsider the dominance rule's interaction with noisy top-k retrieval.** With `top_k=10` and `CONTRADICTED` dominating `SUPPORTED`, a single high-confidence false positive among 10 candidates is sufficient to override 9 correctly-neutral results. Whether this is the right tradeoff for short claims against large corpora vs. requiring corroboration (e.g., ≥2 contradicting chunks) is a measurement worth running — but is an architecture question, correctly out of scope for this evaluation-only study.
3. **Re-examine whether this dataset is the right instrument for verdict accuracy at all**, given the task-definition mismatch identified in Section 5 (loose "fits the character" vs. strict "text entails the claim"). A dataset whose `consistent` examples are deliberately verifiable, source-grounded facts (like the long-narrative validation's hand-built Castaways claims) would isolate NLI/retrieval quality without this confound.
4. **Investigate the wrong-character retrieval cases** (Section 6) — whether this is query-construction sensitivity (the claim text alone, without stronger character-name weighting, isn't always pulling the right character's chunks) or a corpus-density issue (minor characters have less indexed text, more retrieval competition from major characters).
5. Retrieval and fusion metrics remain unmeasurable on this dataset; if accuracy work continues here, adding even minimal gold evidence spans (reusing the existing `GoldSpan`/`GoldExample` schema, as in `datasets/phase6_gold.jsonl` and `datasets/castaways_smoke_claims.jsonl`) would unlock Recall@k/MRR/citation-accuracy and separate retrieval quality from NLI quality directly.
